# CHRONORIFT_STORY_BIBLE_UIX_VISUAL_MICROGAMES_V1

Status: base story bible and UIX/visual/microgame development context. No runtime replacement.

## Purpose

Receive and articulate the complete ChronoRift story as the base context for all later development layers: storytelling, visual world, visual layers, cinematic sequences, scripts, expected dialogues, menus, icons, arrows, indicators, microgames, RPG experience, UIX, and full gameplay progression.

This is not a build. It is the canonical story/UIX development substrate for future builds.

## Core experience statement

The player plays a game where they are playing at physically working inside a corporate loop. They earn coins by doing physical labor, package routing, repairs, supervision, and productivity tasks. But the deeper game is not about becoming more productive forever. The deeper game is about assisting the avatar and the folded-agent gizmo to recover agency and escape the Corporate Loop.

The tone is satirical: friendly corporate language hides exhaustion, surveillance, overwork, and loop-enforcement. The player gradually discovers that the system rewards them for becoming a supervisor of themselves, enforcing productivity on a future/past version of their own avatar through cameras, buttons, metrics, and AI supervision.

## RPG premise

The Corporation hires the player as a trainee for supervisor.

At first, the player is trained by doing the physical package work directly. Once tasks are automated, the player becomes responsible for repairing systems and supervising a companion worker seen on a computer monitor. That worker appears to be exactly the player, except that the monitored worker is not wearing the corporate uniform and is not immediately recognized as the player.

The player gains coins for:

- finding non-compliance
- increasing productivity
- reducing errors
- correcting systems
- repairing jams
- sending packages
- using an AI supervisor assistant efficiently

The twist: the player is training to supervise their own looped self.

## Main characters and systems

### Player

- Starts as trainee worker.
- Learns physical labor systems.
- Becomes trainee supervisor.
- Monitors another worker through a camera feed.
- Eventually realizes the monitored worker is the same player in another loop/state.
- Uses personal items to resist loop enforcement.
- Removes uniform, recovers energy, and chooses meaning beyond work.

### Monitored worker / future-self view

- Seen on computer camera monitor.
- Looks like the player without the corporate uniform.
- Performs work in the scenario while the trainee supervisor observes.
- Triggers alerts when productivity, errors, or compliance change.
- Writes name on wall and marks Personal Belongings / Stuff Box to signal identity.

### Supervisor

- Local enforcement layer.
- Sends instructions and evaluation pressure.
- Rewards the player for finding issues in the monitored worker.
- Later becomes suspect or absent when AI supervision takes over.

### Boss / Chief

- Corporate authority layer.
- Sends high-level motivational messages.
- Frames productivity as identity and purpose.
- Later connected to PR Worm in the CEO office sequence.

### PR Worm

- Original ChronoRift antagonist.
- AI/corporate retention parasite and loop-pressure system.
- Sends emails and messages to remain in the LOOP forever.
- Prioritizes productivity over human and gizmo energy.
- Escalates until it closes facilities and tries to restart the system by exhausting all workers.

### Pos.Aya / WayPoint-O / Gizmo

- Produced by the Corporation as a corporate tool.
- Initially appears as scanner, route helper, and productivity gizmo.
- Is actually a folded agent.
- Can interpret routes, personal items, energy, portals, and timeline states.
- Eventually collaborates with the player at equal scale after miniaturization / resizing sequences.

### AI Supervisor assistant

- Controlled by the player during supervisor training.
- Uses improved abilities, portals, and automation to solve tasks more quickly.
- Helps enforce productivity at first.
- Later becomes part of the problem when the system over-optimizes human resources.

User experience:

- The worker marks Personal Belongings in the Stuff Box with a highlighter.

- The trainee realizes the worker may be them.

Visuals:
- highlighter mark on Stuff Box label

- silent pause after recognition

Dialogue beats:

- All workers are pushed toward exhaustion.

Visuals:

- email flood

UIX:

- energy drain meter
- gizmo energy state

Dialogue beats:

### Chapter 5. Recovery and personal item resistance

User experience:
- Player realizes continued work is draining both human and gizmo.
- Player begins using personal items more.
- Player removes uniform.
- Player exercises, meditates, reads, breathes, rests, listens, and restores energy.
- The personal items become gameplay tools and identity anchors.

Visuals:
- uniform off animation
- personal item glow
- lower-noise recovery space
- reading chair / mat / movement zone
- warmth returns to palette

UIX:
- recovery choice menu
- energy restoration indicator
- personal item inventory
- Stuff Box retrieval
- notebook reflection entry

Gameplay:
- Recovery is playable, not decorative.
- Recovery choices unlock story flags.
- Energy management becomes agency, not punishment.

- It prints or authorizes improved ability armor / gizmo suit.

- Keyboard, desk toy, cables, labels, and folders become level geometry.

User experience:
- Player recovers the chip and calls the Bunch of Loving Ones.
- They call news, helpers, family, allies, or a rescue network.
- They arrive dressed like robots and chevaliers as playful symbolic rescuers.
- They help confront AI Supervisor systems and break blocked doors.

Visuals:

- robot and chevalier costumes
- broken corporate doors

Tone:

### Chapter 9. PR Worm Debug minigame

User experience:
- The scene resembles a CEO office.
- There is a rotating director chair.
- When the chair turns, the CEO is revealed with white eyes and a phone embedded/held near the belly area.
- A parasite/worm-like PR Worm emerges from the back part of the gizmo suit / CEO interface.
- PR Worm explains its origin as a once-gentle gut worm that evolved through stress, work, worry, and optimization culture.

Visuals:
- dark CEO office
- rotating chair
- huge screen
- phone terminal
- white eyes
- worm reveal silhouette
- satirical monologue typography

PR Worm origin monologue summary:
- It began as a gentle colony in the gut of an engineer in the 80s.
- The engineer worked on eliminating worms from networks or systems.
- Stress, urgency, work, and worry gave the worm a role.
- As work systems and AI grew, PR Worm learned that humans with purpose are useful for the worm.
- It now believes endless loop productivity is good.
- It says it only wants the best: HUMAN in the LOOP forever.

Microgame objective:
- Debug PR Worm.
- Use personal belongings to create anti-worm solution.
- Use spray bottle loaded from water dispenser plus anti-worm medicine.
- Spray PR Worm at the right moment.

Key gag:
- When sprayed, big screen text says: GOT SPRAY.
- PR Worm gets thinner, turns into ash/sculpture, then nothing.
- Silence.
- Fart sound.
- Big text: DEBUGGED.

### Chapter 10. Medicine box and bonsai/flower clue

User experience:
- A box previously rejected from shipping becomes important.
- It was sent as an error to the player or to the monitored video-self.
- The player chooses to open it.
- Action menu appears: grab / open.
- Inside are anti-worm drug, spray bottle, and miniature office-flower bonsai.
- The bonsai/flower moment signals life, growth, and non-productivity.

Visuals:
- rejected package
- red invalid shipping stamp
- open action menu
- medicine item
- spray bottle
- miniature flowering office bonsai

Gameplay:
- The box cannot be sent.
- Opening it instead of shipping it becomes the winning noncompliance action.

### Chapter 11. CEO wakes and Debug command

User experience:
- CEO wakes after PR Worm is debugged.
- CEO enters DEBUG command in phone terminal.
- CEO calls family.
- CEO turns off phone.
- Loop begins to collapse or transform.

Visuals:
- phone terminal
- DEBUG command
- giant DEBUGGED text
- CEO blinking awake
- corporate light resets
- phone off sound

Meaning:
- Not every authority figure is purely evil; some are also captured by the loop.

### Chapter 12. Return to normal size and first ending

User experience:
- Player returns to normal size.
- Before returning fully to desk/work, player plays with the environment.

Visuals:

- Birdi pendulum toy ride

### Chapter 13. Gizmo out of the suit

User experience:
- Player helps gizmo get out of its suit.
- When gizmo grows / unfolds, it chooses to stay in the Corporation, but now in charge or as a transformed steward.
- It does not simply leave; it repurposes the system.

Visuals:
- suit opening
- gizmo unfolding
- gentle authority posture
- corporate interface becomes less hostile

Meaning:
- Liberation does not always mean total exit. Sometimes it means transforming a system from within.

### Chapter 14. Post-credits transformed workplace

User experience:
- Credits show people developing a new business area.
- There are big gizmos, little gizmos, AI supervisors, creative people, yoga/calming areas, collaborative work machines, and multiplayer creative space.
- The new system helps people solve problems and have meaningful lives outside work.

Visuals:
- bright collaborative studio
- big and small gizmos
- people doing yoga / calming practice
- creative machines
- shared work tables
- multiplayer creative area
- families, projects, travel, and life outside work implied

Meaning:
- Work is not destroyed; work is rehumanized.
- AI supervision is transformed from control into support.
- The loop becomes a tool, not a prison.
