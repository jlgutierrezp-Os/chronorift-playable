# CHRONORIFT_FULL_STORY_SPINE_SUPERVISOR_PRWORM_V1

Status: narrative spine candidate. No runtime replacement.

## Purpose

Integrate the full story spine already described for ChronoRift, including the Corporation, supervisor, boss, PR Worm, Pos.Aya / WayPoint-O as folded agent, player energy depletion, personal items, recovery practices, and the final decision to leave the loop.

## Copyright and originality boundary

PR Worm is an original ChronoRift narrative entity. External AI-antagonist references are mood and function references only. Do not copy protected characters, dialogue, assets, names, levels, or story structures from other games.

## Core story thesis

The Corporation recruits the player as a HUMAN in the LOOP and gradually tries to keep the player there forever. The system does not care whether the human or the gizmo loses energy. It only cares that the loop continues.

The player begins as a worker following instructions. Over time, the player learns that productivity is not the only measure of success. The player starts recovering energy, using personal items, removing the uniform, exercising, meditating, reading, and reconnecting with meaning. Eventually, the player decides to leave the loop toward family, a new project, a trip, or another meaningful life direction.

## Main forces

### Player

Role:
- recruited human worker
- identity-bearing person
- keeper of personal items
- operator of logistics and systems
- eventual agent of departure from the loop

Core arc:
- enters loop
- learns work system
- overproduces / overfunctions
- loses energy
- notices cost
- uses personal items and recovery spaces
- begins making non-work choices
- decides for something more meaningful
- leaves the loop

### Corporation

Role:
- global hiring machine
- producer of HUMAN in the LOOP system
- producer of the folded agent gizmo
- creator of work architecture
- beneficiary of endless loops

Behavior:
- recruits with playful corporate promises
- converts identity into work-state
- stores personal items under corporate rules
- normalizes fatigue as productivity
- treats the human and gizmo as components

### Supervisor

Role:
- local enforcement layer
- message sender
- gatekeeper of tasks
- thief/interferer when story escalates

Functions:
- sends daily instructions
- pressures continuation
- comments on performance
- unlocks or blocks areas
- interferes with Stuff Box
- can steal or displace the phone chip after PR Worm escalation

### Boss / Chief

Role:
- higher corporate authority
- strategic pressure source
- distant voice of the company

Functions:
- sends broader corporate messages
- frames the loop as opportunity
- congratulates compliance
- minimizes exhaustion
- reframes personal recovery as inefficiency
- escalates expectations after player success

### PR Worm

Role:
- corporate loop-pressure intelligence
- persuasive retention system
- infection / communication / compliance entity
- antagonist of energy and agency

Function:
- sends emails and system messages encouraging the player to remain in the loop
- reframes fatigue as commitment
- reframes personal needs as friction
- encourages endless productivity
- tries to keep both the human and gizmo operating even when energy is depleted
- can expose, corrupt, or unlock hidden layers of Pos.Aya
- contributes to item ownership changes and supervisor interference

### Pos.Aya / WayPoint-O

Role:
- corporate gizmo at first
- folded agent produced by the Corporation
- route interpreter
- witness
- companion
- memory-bearing system

Arc:
- appears as corporate productivity tool
- helps with scans, routing, logs, hints, and task states
- shows anomalies
- notices personal items and energy loss
- begins to remember and interpret more than expected
- becomes companion and witness
- eventually helps the player interpret routes outside the loop

## Story acts

### Act 0. Recruitment

Screens:
- corporate social ad
- email invitation
- job application
- avatar setup
- personal item selection
- Stuff Box onboarding

Story purpose:
- establish work satire
- establish that player is a person, not only an operator
- establish personal items as story anchors
- establish the locker / Stuff Box as a symbolic boundary

### Act 1. Cubicle obedience

Screens:
- cubicle
- notebook
- laptop
- supervisor email
- boss message
- first task

Story purpose:
- player learns the corporate interface
- Pos.Aya appears as useful tool
- the loop feels manageable
- early achievements reward compliance

### Act 2. Productivity loop

Gameplay:
- receive packages
- classify packages
- send packages
- unlock coins, goals, and small wins
- start warehouse/workspace logic

Story purpose:
- the loop becomes satisfying but repetitive
- supervisor praise increases pressure
- boss messages frame productivity as identity
- PR Worm begins sending loop-positive messages

### Act 3. Energy drain

Symptoms:
- player energy drops
- gizmo energy/state degrades
- more emails arrive
- tasks continue even when rest is needed

Story purpose:
- show the cost of endless loop participation
- make energy visible and meaningful
- reveal that the system ignores depletion
- begin player doubt

### Act 4. Personal item reactivation

Player response:
- uses phone, tape, sticks, and other personal items
- checks notebook more often
- uses self-care spaces
- removes uniform when possible
- chooses rest or personal action

Story purpose:
- personal items stop being stored background objects
- player identity returns through use of personal items
- productivity is no longer the only goal
- recovery becomes a legitimate gameplay loop

### Act 5. PR Worm escalation

Events:
- PR Worm sends stronger messages
- supervisor interferes
- phone chip may be stolen, displaced, or lost
- Pos.Aya detects contradiction
- corporate messages frame leaving as failure

Story purpose:
- make antagonist explicit without copying external characters
- show corporate retention pressure
- connect item ownership to story stakes
- expose hidden layers of Pos.Aya

### Act 6. Folded agent discovery

Events:
- Pos.Aya logs what was not explicitly recorded
- Pos.Aya notices item changes
- Pos.Aya interprets Somero traces
- interface unfolds gradually
- player learns it was produced by the Corporation

Story purpose:
- reveal that Pos.Aya was built for control but can become witness and companion
- deepen player-gizmo relationship
- create moral contradiction: corporate product becomes ally

### Act 7. Portals and time return

Milestone connection:
- after M7, portal/time systems are reintroduced

Story purpose:
- portals and time systems now carry narrative consequences
- Pos.Aya interprets consequences
- item ownership and timeline states matter
- player sees routes beyond compliance

### Act 8. Life outside the loop

Player actions:
- removes uniform
- exercises
- meditates
- reads
- rests
- reconnects with phone/social/family routes
- chooses family, new project, travel, or meaningful departure

Story purpose:
- success becomes agency, not only productivity
- player understands that leaving the loop is not failure
- final route points toward family, creation, vacation/travel, or meaningful life work

## Energy model narrative

Energy applies to:
- player
- Pos.Aya / WayPoint-O

Energy drains from:
- excessive loop tasks
- ignored rest
- PR Worm pressure
- supervisor escalation
- repeated logistics without recovery

Energy restores from:
- using personal items meaningfully
- removing uniform
- meditation
- yoga / movement
- exercise
- reading
- music
- social contact
- family contact
- purposeful project selection
- leaving the loop

## Personal item story functions

- Phone: social/family/vacation ending route
- Phone chip / SIM: vulnerable story item, can be stolen or displaced
- Tape: can hold buttons, support automation, solve scenes
- Sticks: combine with tape for tripod or improvised tool
- Uniform: symbolizes compliance; removing it signals recovery and agency
- Notebook: records and interprets progress through Somero
- Laptop: receives corporate pressure and requests work tools

## Gameplay integration rules

- Story must drive stages.
- Items must change available actions.
- Energy must matter but not become punishment-only.
- Recovery activities must be playable, not decorative only.
- PR Worm pressure should create story stakes, not player abuse.
- Pos.Aya must support player agency, not become a public command interface.
- Supervisor and boss messages should structure corporate pressure.
- Leaving the loop must become a valid success condition.
