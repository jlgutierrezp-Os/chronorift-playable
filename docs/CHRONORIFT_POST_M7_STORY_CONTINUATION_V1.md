# CHRONORIFT_POST_M7_STORY_CONTINUATION_V1

Status: sequencing addendum for CHRONORIFT_SERIOUS_DEVELOPMENT_PROGRAM_V1. No runtime replacement.

## Core correction

M7 is not the end of the game program. After M7 reintroduces portals and time systems, the story continues.

The previous feedback/update cadence remains required, but it must not replace story progression. Feedback cadence becomes a support layer that wraps each milestone.

## Corrected milestone order after M7

### M7. Portals and time systems reintroduction

Goal: reintroduce ChronoRift identity after stable RPG, logistics, package, and construction foundations.

Required result:
- dedicated portal surfaces work
- deterministic portal transforms work
- item and box state is preserved
- time/rift prototype exists
- story can now use portal/time consequences

### M8. Story continuation after ChronoRift unlock

Goal: continue the narrative after the player understands the portal/time system.

Story functions:
- reveal why HUMAN in the LOOP is being kept in the loop
- escalate the supervisor and boss conflict
- develop Pos.Aya / WayPoint-O as companion and witness
- expose PR Worm consequences
- recover, lose, or track personal items through story states
- connect logistics systems to corporate exploitation and player freedom
- open routes toward social, family, vacation, or life-purpose endings

Deliverables:
- post-M7 cinematic scenes
- achievement-triggered dialogue
- new boss/supervisor messages
- Pos.Aya contextual dialogue
- PR Worm event chain
- item ownership reversals
- story flags visible in notebook / Somero
- new stage goals driven by story, not only mechanics

Acceptance:
- player receives a clear narrative reason to continue after portals/time unlock
- at least one story decision changes flags
- at least one personal item changes state or owner
- notebook/Somero records the story continuation

### M9. Advanced RPG and life-outside-the-loop arc

Goal: move beyond workplace automation toward meaning, agency, and leaving the loop.

Deliverables:
- social/family/vacation route setup
- phone or communication item becomes narratively important
- work-life contrast areas unlock
- optional self-care or social areas gain function
- player decisions affect ending path

Acceptance:
- player understands that success is not only productivity
- at least one path points outside the corporate loop
- personal item choices matter to the ending arc

### M10. Feedback/update cadence as permanent governance layer

Goal: keep every player feedback cycle structured and safe.

This milestone does not replace story. It wraps all milestones.

Deliverables:
- feedback form
- issue update template
- update proposal template
- human approval gate
- safe point registry update

Acceptance:
- feedback produces structured update proposal before new build work
- no public feedback can directly modify repo or runtime

## Narrative rule

After M7, every new mechanic must answer:

- What story beat does this unlock?
- What player choice does this enable?
- What item, relationship, or identity state changes?
- How does the notebook/Somero record it?
- Does this help the player move toward agency outside the loop?

## Development rule

Do not let the build become only a sandbox of systems. The RPG story spine must drive stage progression after M7.

## Current next implication

Before implementing post-M7 content, stabilize M0-M2, then M3-M7. Once M7 passes, continue into M8 story continuation rather than stopping at feedback infrastructure.
