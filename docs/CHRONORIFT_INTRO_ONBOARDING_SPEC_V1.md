# CHRONORIFT_INTRO_ONBOARDING_SPEC_V1

Status: design specification. No runtime replacement.

## Purpose

Define the first playable narrative onboarding sequence before the logistics workspace. The game should not begin in abstract box transport; it begins with a corporate story, player identity, personal items, and Stuff Box ritual.

## Narrative premise

A global corporation recruits HUMAN in the LOOP workers with no experience and expects the human to stay in the loop forever.

## Required screens

### 1. Corporate recruitment animation

- Corporate ad appears in a professional/social work feed or email.
- The company describes easy work, no experience needed, and endless human participation.
- Tone: playful, readable, darkly comic, corporate-satirical.
- Output: player chooses to apply.

### 2. Job application

- Player sees a simple form.
- Player chooses a name or provisional identity.
- Player confirms application.
- The corporation accepts quickly.

### 3. Avatar setup

- Player selects an avatar from neutral choices.
- Player adds distinguishing features.
- Player chooses personal items.
- Avatar choices should be visual and recoverable later.

### 4. Personal item selection

Initial item classes:

- phone
- phone chip or SIM
- adhesive tape
- sticks
- additional story items as needed

Rules:

- Items are not only cosmetic.
- Items can later solve RPG scenes.
- Items can later unlock endings or story transitions.
- Item ownership must be tracked.

### 5. Stuff Box / locker

- Player opens locker.
- Player places personal items in Stuff Box.
- Player sees a corporate instruction note.
- Player closes locker.
- Closing locker starts Stage 1.

## RPG item implications

- Phone: later supports social/family/vacation ending.
- Phone chip: can be lost or stolen after PR Worm infection.
- Tape: can hold buttons down or solve office automation scenes.
- Sticks: can combine with tape to create a tripod.

## Acceptance

- Intro is understandable without reading a long manual.
- Player understands why they are at the corporation.
- Avatar and items feel meaningful.
- Stuff Box is clearly a physical story object.
- Closing locker creates a strong transition into the cubicle.

## Not in scope

- Public account creation.
- Database-backed identity.
- Public modification of game state.
- Core runtime replacement without human approval.
