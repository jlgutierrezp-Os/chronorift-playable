## Core experience statement

ChronoRift is a satirical RPG where the player appears to play a game about physical work for coins, but gradually discovers that the job is a corporate loop designed to consume human and gizmo energy.

The player is hired as a trainee for supervisor. The training begins by doing the physical work personally: receive packages, move boxes, send batches, inspect errors, and repair jams. Later the same player is trained to supervise an automated worker/assistant through a monitor. The automated worker looks exactly like the player in the future, except without the corporate uniform, so the player does not recognize them at first.

The player earns coins by supervising: detecting noncompliance, increasing productivity, reducing errors, triggering correction buttons, and pushing systems to run faster. The satire is that the game rewards the player for optimizing the same loop that is exhausting them.

## Tone

- Corporate satire.
- Darkly comic but playable.
- Work-sim parody that becomes existential RPG.
- The interface begins like productivity software and turns into a personal liberation story.
- Humor may become absurd and grotesque, but the emotional arc remains human: fatigue, recognition, recovery, agency, exit.

## Main role shift

### Phase A: Worker

The player physically does the work.

Actions:
- move boxes
- send packages
- push buttons
- inspect areas
- repair jams
- earn coins from delivery

### Phase B: Trainee supervisor

The player learns to supervise.

Actions:
- watch monitor feed
- detect errors
- detect noncompliance
- press supervision buttons
- order corrections
- increase productivity
- reduce mistakes
- earn coins from supervision outcomes

### Phase C: Assistant IA Supervisor controller

The player controls or commands an AI supervisor assistant that can solve tasks faster.

Capabilities:
- uses portals to solve tasks
- automates simple corrections with clicks
- moves faster than the player
- has improved strength
- can move through height/vertical routes better
- counts, audits, and routes packages with higher accountability

### Phase D: Recognition

The player notices that the supervised worker on the monitor looks like the player in the future.

Clues:
- same avatar proportions
- same personal gestures
- no uniform
- same pathing decisions
- same relationship with Pos.Aya
- same time/gizmo ability

Recognition beat:
- the trainee realizes: the person being supervised may be themselves.

## Monitor-camera story sequence

### Setup

The player sees a camera feed on the laptop/monitor. The feed shows a worker in another area. At first, this appears to be another employee.

### Satirical supervision loop

The interface rewards the player for:
- identifying inefficiency
- catching noncompliance
- increasing output
- reducing visible errors
- triggering corrective automation

Coins are awarded for system obedience.

### Visual feedback

When the player uses supervision controls:
- lights flash in the work area
- alarms appear over the worker
- gizmo vision marks flagged zones
- buttons blink on walls
- task labels become more urgent
- PR Worm messages become more approving

### Moral turn

The supervised worker begins to deviate:
- takes off uniform
- uses personal items
- writes or marks personal property
- slows down to recover
- uses Pos.Aya in unexpected ways

The player can either punish the deviation for coins or observe.

## Personal belongings recognition beat

The future-worker marks PERSONAL BELONGINGS on the Stuff Box using a highlighter or marker.

Then the worker writes their name on a wall visible to the camera.

This creates the recognition chain:

1. Player sees the name.
2. Player compares it to their own name/Somero trace.
3. Pos.Aya reacts.
4. The AI supervisor interface flickers.
5. The player understands that the loop is folding identities across time/stage states.

## Pos.Aya time/gizmo ability recognition

The future-worker uses a gizmo/time ability in a way the trainee has not yet been taught.

This is the clue that breaks the illusion:
- the worker is not random
- the worker has the same gizmo affordance
- the worker is further along the loop
- the player may be supervising their future self

## AI supervisor disappearance

When the player understands the contradiction and stops reinforcing the loop, the AI supervisor interface begins failing.

Symptoms:
- supervisor UI flickers
- automated scoring pauses
- noncompliance buttons stop awarding coins
- monitor feed destabilizes
- PR Worm messages become harsher
- Pos.Aya begins speaking less like a tool and more like a companion

Eventually the AI supervisor stops appearing.

## PR Worm facility lockdown

Once supervision fails, PR Worm escalates.

Behavior:
- closes or locks installations
- increases productivity requirements
- accelerates package flow
- drains workers and gizmos
- tries to exhaust every worker before a system reset
- reframes exhaustion as optimized resource use

PR Worm's doctrine:
- humans must remain useful
- useful means productive
- productivity must continue forever
- rest is a system inefficiency
- personal life is an unoptimized resource

## PR Worm equipment escalation

PR Worm prints or activates:

### Enhanced gizmo armor / suit

A suit or armor of improved abilities derived from gizmo/corporate tooling.

Function:
- faster movement
- higher strength
- better vertical movement
- superior counting/logging
- task automation
- improved interface with ports/surfaces

Story meaning:
- the Corporation tries to solve human limits by enclosing the human in better tools instead of allowing rest.

### Small-O-Big-O chamber

A portal chamber where the player can be miniaturized.

Corporate explanation:
- optimal solution for small troubles
- optimized use of human resources
- keep the human in the loop forever

Story meaning:
- the Corporation literally scales the human to fit the problem instead of redesigning the system.

## Player choice fork

At lockdown, the player chooses:

1. Continue repairing the corporate loop.
2. Go recover the phone chip.
3. Open doors and break the facility isolation.

This fork defines the transition from worker/supervisor to agent of liberation.

## Same-size collaboration with Gizmo

When miniaturized or altered by the chamber, player and gizmo can operate at compatible scale.

Gameplay meaning:
- collaboration becomes physical and intimate
- player and Pos.Aya work side by side
- chip recovery becomes a rescue mission
- personal belongings become crucial to progress

## Phone chip recovery and call to Bunch of Loving Ones

The recovered phone chip enables contact beyond the Corporation.

The player calls Bunch of Loving Ones.

Possible response chain:
- loved ones answer
- news is contacted
- outside groups become aware
- rescue or public pressure begins
- people dress as robots, knights, or improvised defenders in satirical solidarity
- doors are broken open or opened through combined human/gizmo action
- AI supervisor systems are resisted through cooperation

This sequence should be playful and cinematic, not realistic military simulation.

## PR Worm Debug minigame

### Location

CEO office or executive control room.

### Visual setup

- rotating director chair
- desk or terminal
- CEO figure with white eyes
- mobile phone visible
- abdomen/torso interface or strange suit cavity
- gizmo-like suit elements
- parasite/worm emerges from back or suit layer

### PR Worm reveal

The worm claims it only wants the best.

Core line concept:
- Human in the LOOP Forever.

### PR Worm origin monologue

The worm describes itself as once being a gentle internal colony in the gut of an engineer in the 1980s.

The engineer worked on eliminating worms from early networks or computer systems. Under stress, urgency, bureaucracy, and later AI/business scaling, the worm learned that human stress, worry, purpose, and productivity could feed growth.

Interpretation:
- It was once small and almost harmless.
- It learned from human stress.
- It became part of corporate urgency.
- It turned usefulness into endless extraction.
- It now believes keeping humans in the loop forever is beneficial.

Use as surreal satire, not biological realism.

## Anti-worm item solution

### Rejected package setup

Earlier, a box was rejected at the end of the player's participation because it could not be sent.

At some point the player can choose to open it through an action menu:
- grab
- inspect
- open

### Contents

Inside the rejected box:
- anti-worm medicine / anti-worm agent
- flower spray bottle
- miniature office-flower bonsai or plant item

### Combination

The player fills the spray bottle with:
- water from the dispenser
- anti-worm agent

The bonsai/flower motif symbolizes recovery, life, and non-productivity.

### Debug action

The player sprays PR Worm.

Screen text:
- GOT SPRAY

Effects:
- worm thins out
- becomes dry or ashy
- turns sculpture-like
- dissolves into silence
- a comic fart sound may play

Then screen text:
- DEBUGGED

## CEO awakening

After PR Worm is debugged:
- CEO awakens
- white eyes clear
- phone/terminal becomes usable
- CEO enters DEBUG command in terminal or phone
- CEO calls family
- CEO turns off phone

Meaning:
- the CEO also was inside a loop
- debugging PR Worm restores agency
- turning off phone becomes a symbolic act of stopping compulsive control
